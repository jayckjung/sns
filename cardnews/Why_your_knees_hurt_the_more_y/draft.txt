After a long run, your cadence might look “normal,” but fatigue can quietly make you run stiffer—less knee bend, more impact—and that’s why your knees start complaining 🏃‍♂️🦵.  
Research shows knee flexion drops while peak ground reaction force rises, even when your steps per minute don’t change.  
Remember: your knees can absorb 3–4× your body weight (and in some cases far more), so small form shifts add up fast ⚠️.  
Stop overstriding—don’t land way out in front of your center, because that impact goes straight into the knee.  
Aim for a smooth 170–180 cadence, warm up 5–10 minutes, and give recovery the same respect as the workout ✅💪.
