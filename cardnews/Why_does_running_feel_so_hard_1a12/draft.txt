If running feels harder than it should, check your “bounce” — vertical oscillation might be draining your energy 🏃‍♂️⬆️⬇️.  
Too much up-and-down means you’re wasting effort lifting your body instead of moving forward, which hurts running economy and makes fatigue hit sooner ⚡️.  
A solid target is roughly 2–4 inches per stride (many elites stay under 3), and yes—too high or too low can both cost you efficiency 📏.  
Track it with 3D IMU data using Garmin HRM-Pro, or even AirPods with the StrideCoach app.  
Then clean it up: nudge cadence toward 170–180, keep a slight forward lean from the ankles, and build core/glute/leg strength for a steadier, faster stride 💪.
