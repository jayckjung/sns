If you’re a new runner, that “unwanted guest” mid-run is usually an injury—and up to 85% of beginners deal with one each year. 😣  
Here’s the good news: Dr. Zoe Y. S. Chan’s team tested gait retraining in 320 new runners, and a simple 2-week, feedback-based program cut injury risk by 62%. 📉  
Why it works: gait retraining lowered vertical loading rate—less impact force every time your foot hits the ground. 🦶  
Less impact = less fatigue and fewer issues like patellofemoral pain and plantar fasciitis. ✅  
Want running that lasts? Start training your form, not just your fitness. 🏃‍♂️
