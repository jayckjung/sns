Your marathon hydration goal isn’t “drink more”—it’s to avoid both too little and too much 💧⚖️. I want you to keep in-race weight loss under 2% (ACSM), because dehydration can spike heart rate and body temp and crush your endurance 🏃‍♂️🔥. But overdrinking isn’t safer—hyponatremia is real, and Boston Marathon data showed it in 13% of finishers. Start smart: 2–4 hours pre-race aim for ~0.08–0.16 fl oz per lb, then during the race use a range like 14–27 fl oz/hour and adjust for heat, pace, and aid stations. Personalize it by tracking pre/post-run weight, and plan sodium + carbs (30–60 g/hour, up to 90 g/hour for 2.5+ hours) so your fluids actually work for you.
